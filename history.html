Department History

Proudly shaping textile excellence since 1960

The department of Textile Materials Engineering was founded in 1960, to meet the increasing demand of know-how people in textile dyeing industries of Bangladesh. The graduates from this department obtained top-notch position in their professions. The department reflects the dream and commitment of BUTEX in engaging research activities and collaboration with industry. The academic learning of the department includes strong practical programs such as laboratory experiments, internships, in-house projects, joint projects with industries. The course curriculum embraces fibre-forming polymer engineering, fibre characterization, as well as wet treatment and coloration of different forms of textiles such as fibre, yarn, fabric, and garments, etc. and textile finishing that involved to improve aesthetic and service value of textile goods.

The aim of the department is to promote employ-ability, entrepreneurship, leadership, research aptitude among the students to troubleshoot the industrial problems and developments of new technology without leaving footprint in environment and society.

  • Strong focus on laboratory experiments and industry internships.
  • Collaboration with industries through joint projects.
  • Curriculum covering fibre-forming polymer engineering, fibre characterization, wet treatment, coloration, and textile finishing.
  • Emphasis on employability, entrepreneurship, leadership, and research aptitude.
  • Commitment to environmental and social responsibility in technological development.
“Empowering minds, weaving innovation, and building the future of textiles.”

Key Advantages of Studying in TME at BUTEX

  • Cutting-edge textile laboratories with advanced dyeing & finishing equipment.
  • Strong linkage with leading textile industries for research & internships.
  • Expert faculty with global research exposure and industry experience.
  • Hands-on training in fibre analysis, coloration, and finishing processes.
  • Focus on sustainability and eco-friendly textile innovations.
