🌊 Advanced Research Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy (FTIR)

Identifies chemical bonds and functional groups in materials with high precision infrared spectra.

Overview

The ABB MB3000 is a robust and reliable laboratory FT-IR (Fourier Transform Infrared) spectrometer designed for high-performance and minimal maintenance. It's known for its permanently aligned optical system, modular design, and long-life components, making it suitable for various applications, including research, quality control, and industrial development. The instrument comes with the Horizon MB software suite, offering flexibility for a wide range of analyses.

The MB3000 boasts a permanently aligned optical system with fixed components, eliminating the need for adjustments and ensuring consistent, reproducible results. It also features a solid-state laser instead of a HeNe laser and non-hygroscopic optics, reducing the need for purging.

The modular architecture allows for easy maintenance and potential field replacement of components, contributing to its long-term usability. The Horizon MB software suite provides a user-friendly interface for data acquisition, processing, and analysis, with options for library searching and compliance with 21 CFR Part 11.

📸 Workflow & Gallery

Step 1: Sample Preparation

Proper preparation of textile or polymer samples before FTIR measurement.

Step 2: Spectrum Collection

Collect infrared absorption spectra to detect molecular vibrations and bonds.

Step 3: Spectrum Processing

Use Horizon MB software to process, baseline correct, and analyze spectra.

Step 4: Data Interpretation

Identify functional groups, compare with reference libraries, and generate reports.
